Bubble Tea

Description

Bubble tea, also known as boba tea, is a popular Taiwanese drink that combines sweet, flavored tea with chewy tapioca pearls. It originated in the 1980s and has since become a worldwide phenomenon. The drink typically consists of brewed tea, milk or fruit flavors, sweeteners like sugar or honey, and the signature tapioca pearls. It's often served cold, sometimes blended with ice to create a slushy texture, making it a refreshing and indulgent treat.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup of dried tapioca pearls
  • 1/2 cup of brewed tea (black, green, or fruit-flavored)
  • 1/4 cup of milk or non-dairy alternative
  • Sweetener (sugar, honey, or syrup) to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the tapioca pearls according to the package instructions. Once cooked, rinse them under cold water and set aside.
  2. Brew the tea and let it cool to room temperature.
  3. In a glass, combine the brewed tea, milk, sweetener, and any additional flavorings. Stir well to mix.
  4. Add a handful of ice cubes to the glass and top with the cooked tapioca pearls.
  5. Insert a wide straw into the glass to enjoy the tapioca pearls along with the tea. Mix well before drinking to combine all the flavors.
